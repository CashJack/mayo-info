CURRICULUM

The school is affiliated to the Central Board of Secondary Education; yet has created its own unique curriculum till class VIII. This 'Open Ended ' and ‘Concept Driven’ curriculum takes the best from the various curricula available to create a comprehensive framework that encourages creativity and fosters learning concepts in a seamless fashion. 􏰀The medium of instruction is English, however the school lays equal emphasis on Hindi, Sanskrit, Punjabi, French and German. 􏰀All three Streams - Science, Commerce and Humanities are offered to classes XI & XII with a variety of optional combination of subjects.

PEDAGOGY- FACULTY THAT KEEPS ABREAST WITH THE BEST PEDAGOGIES

The school emphasizes on keeping students at the centre of the learning process and uses the three pillars - Technology, Teacher & Campus in a collaborative manner to make the learning process a gainful & enjoyable experience for students.

TECHNOLOGY AT MAYO

At Mayo College, we consider technology as a tool that is to be used judiciously in education. While we invest in and have access to the latest technology on campus, we monitor its use closely. Students are granted regulated access to devices. At the same time, we use technology to make learning fun and engaging. All our classrooms are equipped with the most advanced interactive boards and have full Wi Fi connectivity. While the whole campus is equipped with modern technology, here are a few technological highlights of the students experience at Mayo
• The Interactive 3D virtual lab : Provides students an opportunity to enter into a life like-virtual world, where abstract ideas come to life. Students are taught the complex concepts of Science and Social Science through 3D capsules.
• The Robotics Lab Robotics training program for students of Class VIII-X emphasizes on making Robots from scratch using locally procured materials instead of the ubiquitous plug and play model. Students are taught how to program the Robots using C++ and Python.
• The Dobsonian Telescope at Mayo College allows students to witness many astronomical events and celestial bodies in the night sky. Students study the Moon, view Jupiter's Moons, the Crescent Venus, Saturn's rings and watch the Eclipses when they occur.
• 3D Scanner & Printer Mayo College is equipped with a 3D scanner and printer. Students from class 7th onwards are taught to feed models into the computer to create three dimensional solid objects.
• Drone Photography Students are taught Aerial Photographyusing professional drones available with the school. They hone their skills by photographing numerous school functions.

CAMPUS AS A CLASSROOM - Education Outside Campus

The large campus is being used as a tool in teaching. For example the museum has been incorporated as a rich resource for teaching subjects. Age specific Tool Kits have been created by museologists to allow various subjects and topics to be taught in an interesting manner using the unique artifacts available. present at the museum. Teachers are encouraged to take lessons outdoors and to use the facilities present on the campus to develop practical understanding of the theories being studied.
At Mayo, we recognize that the world is shrinking and strive to provide our students rich experiences from outside the campus.

EDUCATION MANTRAS WE FOLLOW

• Leveraging Technology: Teachersusedthelatest technologyto teach students 21st Century skills and enhance their learning experience. At Mayo we believe in being technology enabled but not technology enslaved.
• Using campus as a classroom: Lessons at Mayo are held in learning spaces and are not bound to classrooms. The large campus is being used as a tool in teaching. For example, the Museum has been incorporated as a rich resource for teaching subjects. Age specific Tool Kits have been created by Museologists to enable this.
• Teacher as a Guide and a Mentor: Moving on from being the 'Know it all' to becoming a facilitator. The role of a teacher at Mayo today is that of a mentor, who strives to inspire the child. The goal is of making all class room interactions 'Student Centric'.
• Inter- disciplinary Team Teaching: We are cognizant of the fact that boundaries between subjects are artificial. At Mayo we teach concepts and not 'subjects'! Our faculty has mapped content across disciplines and often delivers concepts by workingtogetherin inter-disciplinaryteams.
• Continuous Assessment: Ideal blend of Formative & Summative assessment to ease stress; complete transparency and access assured.

GIVING STUDENTS THE INTERNATIONAL EDGE

The Vision Statement of Mayo College is to “Build on tradition and prepare global leaders with character”. The world is increasingly becoming boundary-less and if a boy has to succeed, he should have an appreciation of global affairs and be able to function comfortably in any part of the world. To give our students an international perspective, every year we send a certain percentage of them for exchange programmes and projects overseas to countries like Australia, South Africa, Germany, France, USA and UK.s
