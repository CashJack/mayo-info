EXCHANGE PROGRAMME


FOR STUDENTS

Our students wear 'Global Boots' but remain proud of their 'Indian Roots'

Every year more than 100 Mayo students (Approximately 15% of the Mayo student body) are a part of international student exchanges and almost all our students participate in some student exchanges or service programs from the wide range we offer.
Round Square: As a member of the Round Square, Mayo College has student exchanges with schools in Australia and South Africa; service projects in Jordan, Laos, Vietnam, South Africa, Morocco, Thailand, Borneo & Sri Lanka. Otherstudent Exchange Programs: We also have direct student exchange programs with schools in Germany, France, Australia, USA and United Kingdom. Under the exchange programs, boys from both the Junior and Senior School spend 14 days to three months with the host schools/families overseas. As a reciprocal gesture, students from these schools are hosted by Mayo College.Theschoolhas oneofthelargestnumberof inbound foreign exchange students in the country.
Sports centric student exchanges take place every year for Football, Riding etc to countries like England, Australia, Egypt and Russia.

PEDAGOGY

FACULTY THAT KEEPS ABREAST WITH THE BEST PEDAGOGIES

Effective learning also requires great teachers. Mayo College takes pride in its experienced and extremely competent faculty. A lot of emphasis is laid on Faculty Development. Besides regular In-House training, our faculty regularly participates in workshops, seminars and orientation programmes in India and abroad, conducted by CBSE, IPSC, IIT and IIM. Every year the school invites leading experts to deliver lectures and training sessions for our staff with the aims of ensuring that they remain updated with the latest trends in education and that their Soft Skills are constantly enhanced.

TECHNOLOGY AT MAYO

We have an Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Robotics Lab on campus where students are taught how to make robots from scratch and then to program them using C++ and Python.
The school is also equipped with a 3 D scanner and printer. Students are taught how to feed models into the computer to create three dimensional solid objects. 3 D printing (also called additive manufacturing) is already being used to make everything from homes to aircraft and its use is likely to expand in the coming years.
